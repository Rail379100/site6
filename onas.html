ABOUT ME AND THE TEAM


INTRODUCTION BY THE FOUNDER


My name is Monica Perez and I was born in the city of Zaragoza, in the north-east of Spain. In Zaragoza I studied economics at the university and at the same time completed my music studies at the Teruel Conservatory with a specialization in piano.

During this time I was always connected to associations for young people, in which I worked as a catechist, animator and group leader and took part in social activities with street children, gypsies, senior citizens, prisoners, etc.

Image: @landofportraits

It was my professional goal early on to support people in difficult life situations and to work to improve their living conditions.

That is why I specialized in international cooperation and humanitarian aid and completed a master’s degree at the Universidad de Comillas in Madrid. After completing my studies, I worked for the Spanish Embassy and Caritas Española, among others.

This job has given me the opportunity to travel to many different countries and gain experience in these cultures, especially in Latin America and Asia.

I moved to Switzerland at the age of 35. This is where my two sons were born. Becoming a mother was a turning point in my professional life. I had to reinvent myself to reconcile work and motherhood

That's why I started working as a Spanish teacher for adults in “Spanish Studio” in Lucerne. Based on my extensive experience as a mother in a bilingual environment and with family integration as a goal, I developed “Spanish for Children”.

In February 2019 I completed the training as “playgroup leader” at IG Bildung in Lucerne and I am continuing to learn about the theory and practice of child pedagogy. In 2020 I completed the children's teacher course for ELE (Español como Lengua Extranjera) from ELE Intenational and Universidad Europea Miguel de Cervantes.

At the moment I am continuing my education by taking various courses with a focus on pedagogy.

MESSAGE FROM


MONICA


In my life I have learned different languages because of needs and coincidences. With Spanish as my mother tongue, I learned French at school, needed English for my work, German for my family life and Swiss German for my integration in Switzerland.

This experience has shown me that promoting languages in childhood is very beneficial. It is particularly useful for children with a social or family connection to a foreign language. If it is Spanish for you ... I am very happy to assist you.

MESSAGE FROM


JUDITH


I am Mexican and have lived in Switzerland for 5 years. I have a Masters degree in Foreign Language Teaching from the Antonio Betancourt Institute in the State of Yucatán, am a graduate of Total Tourist Technology University of Modern Languages. I have 13 years of teaching experience working with both children and adults.

Before I came to Switzerland, I worked in a private and bilingual school for 6 years. There I was not only a teacher but also a teacher coordinator. This enabled me to see education as one big family: the student, the teacher, the parents and the institution.

I am currently working as a Spanish teacher at the Migros Club School, and I also look after children. I am happy that I am part of Spanish for Children.

In a nutshell ... teaching is my passion!

MESSAGE FROM


NOELIA


My name is Noelia Morell and I come from Elche (Alicante), a city in the southeast of Spain.

I am a child teacher and a specialist in children with special educational needs. I am also the founder of the Educagreens educational project to create healthy schools around the world.

For me, education means giving children wings so they can fly until they reach their dreams. They are capable of ANYTHING if we give them the tools to achieve it.
